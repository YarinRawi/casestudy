01The Story

Design as a hobby.
Then as something serious.

Late nights experimenting with layouts, logos, interfaces — things I was building because I wanted to, not because anyone asked me to. That eventually turned into a decision: stop doing it on the side and commit.

Before design

Four years as a deputy manager in a supermarket chain. That job taught me how to think about systems, people, and what actually matters when things need to work under pressure. I carry that into every project.

STUDIO 6B

A one-year intensive at Studio 6B — learning the full stack of UX/UI: research, wireframing, prototyping, visual design, and working with real briefs. The portfolio here is what came out of it.

02How I Think

Three things I actually believe about design.

Start with the problem

I don't open Figma until I understand what's actually broken. The best-looking interface in the world is useless if it's solving the wrong thing.

Clarity over cleverness

If someone has to think too hard to figure out what to do next, the design failed. Good UX should feel obvious — invisible, even.

Details are the work

Spacing, hierarchy, micro-interactions — these aren't finishing touches. They're how you signal that you actually care about what you made.
